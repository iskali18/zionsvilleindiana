Downtown & Beyond

Brick streets, local shops, cozy cafés, and an unhurried pace — downtown Zionsville is a place to slow down. Just beyond the village, you’ll also find favorite local spots worth a short drive. Whether you’re nearby or visiting for the day, it’s easy to wander, explore, and enjoy the small moments that make the area feel special.

Three easy ways to spend time around town

Easy ideas you can enjoy at your own pace.

Family: fresh air + a sweet finish

Start with an easy, kid-friendly meal at Greek’s Pizzeria, a reliable downtown favorite. From there, head to nearby Lions Park for playgrounds and an easy walking loop. If you’re up for a quieter stretch, continue into the adjacent Creekside Nature Park for a short nature walk. Wrap things up with ice cream at The Scoop.
Stops: Greek’s Pizzeria → Lions Park → Creekside Nature Park → The Scoop

A slow morning wander

Start with coffee at Roasted in the Village or Our Place Coffee — both are best earlier in the day since they close in the early afternoon. Browse Main Street shops at an unhurried pace, then stop at Rosie’s Place for brunch or an early lunch. Before heading home, pick up something sweet (or savory) at Truffles & Creams Chocolatier or Angelo’s Italian Market to enjoy later.
Coffee: Our Place / Roasted → Brunch: Rosie’s → Treats: Truffles or Angelo’s

A relaxed, elevated evening

Choose a sit-down dinner — Noah Grant’s, Convivio Italian Artisan Cuisine, or Cobblestone are all solid options. After dinner, take a slow walk down Main Street, browse shop windows, and enjoy the brick streets after dark. If you want a simple ending, finish with a stop at The Scoop.
Dinner options: Noah Grant’s / Convivio / Cobblestone

Beyond downtown

A few favorites that sit just outside the village core — an easy add-on if you don’t mind a longer walk or a short drive.

Rivet Coffee Bar (Creekside Corporate Park)

A coffee bar serving breakfast and lunch inside Graham Rahal Performance. It’s a fun stop if you enjoy browsing the cars on display while you wait — and it makes for a different kind of “coffee break” than the village cafés.

Schakolad Chocolate Factory (Boone Village)

A dessert-forward stop in Boone Village that’s worth the short drive when you’re in the mood for something sweet. It’s an easy add-on after downtown shopping — especially if you want a treat to take home.

Sub Daze (just beyond Main Street)

A casual, go-to lunch stop a little south of the Main Street stretch. It’s close enough to pair with a downtown visit, but far enough that many visitors won’t spot it while browsing the village core.
