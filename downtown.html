Downtown Zionsville

Brick streets, local shops, cozy cafés, and an unhurried pace — downtown Zionsville is a place to slow down. Whether you’re nearby or visiting for the day, it’s easy to wander, explore, and enjoy the small moments that make the village feel special.

Three easy ways to spend time downtown

Flexible, low-pressure ideas you can adapt to your own pace.

Family
Kids, fresh air, and a sweet finish
Start with an easy meal downtown — Rosie’s Place is a longtime favorite for brunch, while Greek’s Pizzeria is a reliable, kid-friendly option for lunch. From there, head to nearby Lions Park, which has multiple playgrounds and an easy walking loop. If you’re up for a quieter stretch, continue into the adjacent Creekside Nature Park. Wrap things up with ice cream at The Scoop, a favorite stop for families.
Stops: Rosie’s Place or Greek’s Pizzeria → Lions Park → Creekside Nature Park → The Scoop
Casual
A relaxed wander downtown (solo or with friends)
Start with coffee at Our Place Coffee or Roasted in the Village — both are morning spots that wrap up by early afternoon. Take your time browsing Main Street shops at an unhurried pace. For food, Rosie’s Place works well for brunch, while Greek’s Pizzeria is an easy option for lunch. Before heading home, stop by Truffles & Creams Chocolatier or Angelo’s Italian Market to pick up something sweet or savory.
Coffee & food: Our Place / Roasted → Rosie’s Place or Greek’s Pizzeria
Elevated
A relaxed, elevated evening downtown
Choose a sit-down dinner downtown — Noah Grant’s, Convivio Italian Artisan Cuisine, or Cobblestone are all good options. After dinner, take a slow walk down Main Street, browse shop windows, and enjoy the brick streets after dark. If you want a simple, low-key ending, finish the evening with a stop at The Scoop.
Dinner options: Noah Grant’s / Convivio / Cobblestone
