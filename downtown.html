Downtown & Beyond

Brick streets, local shops, cozy cafés, and an unhurried pace — downtown Zionsville is a place to slow down. Just beyond the village, you’ll also find favorite local spots worth a short drive. Whether you’re nearby or visiting for the day, it’s easy to wander, explore, and enjoy the small moments that make the area feel special.

Three easy ways to spend time around town

Easy ideas you can enjoy at your own pace.

Family
Kids, fresh air, and a sweet finish
Start with an easy, kid-friendly meal at Greek’s Pizzeria, a reliable downtown favorite. From there, head to nearby Lions Park, which has multiple playgrounds and an easy walking loop that’s great for burning off energy. If you’re up for a quieter stretch, continue into the adjacent Creekside Nature Park for a short nature walk. Wrap things up with ice cream at The Scoop, a can’t-miss stop for families.
Stops: Rosie’s Place or Greek’s Pizzeria → Lions Park → Creekside Nature Park → The Scoop
Casual
A relaxed wander (solo or with friends)
Start with coffee at Our Place Coffee or Roasted in the Village, both best enjoyed earlier in the day since they close in the early afternoon. Take your time browsing Main Street shops at an unhurried pace. For food, Rosie’s Place is a longtime favorite for brunch or an early lunch. Before heading home, stop by Truffles & Creams Chocolatier or Angelo’s Italian Market to pick up something sweet or savory to enjoy later.
Coffee & food: Our Place / Roasted → Rosie’s Place or Greek’s Pizzeria
Elevated
A relaxed, elevated evening
Choose a sit-down dinner — Noah Grant’s, Convivio Italian Artisan Cuisine, or Cobblestone are all solid options. After dinner, take a slow walk down Main Street, browse shop windows, and enjoy the brick streets after dark. If you want a simple ending, finish the evening with a stop at The Scoop.
Dinner options: Noah Grant’s / Convivio / Cobblestone
