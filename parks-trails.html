Parks & Trails

Zionsville offers a variety of parks, trails, and natural spaces that support different ways of spending time outdoors. This page highlights a selection of places that are well suited for walking, exploring, and enjoying nature at different levels of activity. Additional neighborhood parks and green spaces are woven throughout the community.

Six spots for fresh air, trails, and time outside

A mix of easy loops, natural areas, and longer trail options — all at your own pace.

Lions Park
A central community park with open green space, playground areas, and a paved loop trail around the park. The flat, easy path works well for casual walks, younger kids, strollers, or a quick lap outdoors. It’s a flexible option for low-key time outside.
Starkey Park
One of Zionsville’s largest natural areas, Starkey Park features an extensive network of wooded trails along Eagle Creek. The terrain varies widely, with some routes involving steep ups and downs rather than flat walking paths. Trail intersections and dense woods make attention to markers or a map important. This park is best suited for longer walks and more active hiking.
Zion Nature Sanctuary
A small, protected natural area with walking paths through woods and open habitat. The setting feels quiet and unstructured, with an emphasis on observing nature rather than play equipment. It’s a good fit for visitors and kids who enjoy slower exploration and noticing what’s around them.
Big 4 Rail Trail
A paved, multi-use trail popular with walkers, runners, and cyclists. Long, straight stretches support steady movement rather than wandering, making it well suited for exercise or point-to-point trips. Access points are spread along the route, allowing flexibility in how much distance to cover.
Creekside Nature Park
Located next to Lions Park, Creekside Nature Park offers a more natural setting along the creek. Short trails and open areas encourage informal exploration and time near the water. It pairs well with Lions Park for visitors looking to extend their time outdoors.
Carpenter Nature Preserve (Coming soon)
Coming soon. Carpenter Nature Preserve will add another protected natural space to the community. Details will be shared once the preserve is fully open and ready for visitors.
