The Zionsville Fall Festival is a long-running community tradition and the Lions Club’s largest annual fundraiser, held at Lions Park from September 11–13, 2026. The three-day event features Poor Jack’s Carnival rides, food vendors, and a Saturday morning parade through the Village. Admission to the festival at Lions Park is free, though carnival rides on Saturday and Sunday require the purchase of tickets or a wristband.

The weekend begins Friday evening with a VIP event for families with special needs, offering a more accessible time to enjoy the carnival before it opens to the general public. Saturday centers on the parade through the Village, while Sunday features the American Dream Car Show.

[Visit the Zionsville Lions Club website →](https://www.zionsvillelions.com/fall-festival-parade-2)

## What to expect

Festival events take place at both Lions Park and along Main Street. On Friday, the carnival hosts a 5:00 PM to 8:00 PM VIP event for families with special needs, offering a quieter, more accessible experience.

Saturday is the busiest day, beginning with the 10:00 AM parade through the Village, followed by a full day of activities at Lions Park, including food vendors, community booths, and carnival rides.

Sunday offers a slower pace, with the American Dream Car Show and community bingo rounding out the weekend.

## Plan your visit

**Dates:** September 11–13, 2026  
**Hours:** Friday 5:00 PM – 11:00 PM, Saturday 10:00 AM – 11:00 PM, Sunday 12:00 PM – 5:00 PM  
**Location:** Lions Park, 11053 Sycamore St, Zionsville, IN 46077  
**Admission:** *Free

**The Parade:** The parade begins Saturday at 10:00 AM at Zionsville Community High School and travels through the Village along Main Street, ending near the entrance to Lions Park. Main Street is closed to vehicles during the parade.

**Parking:** On-site parking at Lions Park is available for an $8.00 fee (cash only), which directly supports park maintenance. A public parking lot is located at the northwest corner of **Main Street and Sycamore Road**, with access from both Main Street and First Street. A second lot is located at the southwest corner of **Main Street and Pine Street**. 

Additional parking is available along nearby streets and in public lots throughout the Village. Parking near the parade route and Lions Park fills quickly during the Saturday parade.
