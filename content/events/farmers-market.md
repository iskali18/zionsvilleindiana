The Zionsville Farmers Market is one of the most popular weekly gatherings in downtown Zionsville, drawing locals and visitors to Main Street in the Village every Saturday morning from mid-May through late September. It’s a relaxed Saturday tradition where shoppers can browse local vendors, hear live music, and spend the morning downtown.

## What to expect

Vendors rotate throughout the season but typically include fresh produce from local farms, baked goods, prepared foods, honey, flowers, specialty meats, and handmade goods. Live music is common on market mornings, making it as much a social event as a shopping trip.

## Plan your visit

**Hours:** 8:00 AM – 11:30 AM  
**Location:** 340 S Main St (along Main Street in the Village)  
**Parking:** Main Street is closed to vehicles during market hours. The closest parking lot is at the northwest corner of Main Street and Sycamore Rd., accessible via First Street.  

Parking is also available along Main Street north of the market area, on nearby side streets, and at Lions Park. Spaces close to the market fill quickly after 8:30 AM, so if you prefer to park nearby, aim to arrive around 8:00 when it's less crowded. See the parking and restaurant map near the bottom of this page for public lot locations, street parking areas, and nearby places to eat.

**Dogs:** Welcome on leash.

## Tips from regulars

Arrive before 9 AM for the best selection, especially for popular items like sweet corn and specialty baked goods. Many vendors sell out before closing. Bring a reusable bag and cash — most vendors accept cards, but cash can be faster.

After the market, Main Street shops begin opening around 10 AM, making it easy to continue your morning downtown.

## Where to eat near the market

Several restaurants and coffee spots are a short walk from the market. A few good ways to combine your visit with a meal:

**Coffee & breakfast** — [Roasted in the Village](/businesses/roasted-in-the-village) and [Our Place Coffee](/businesses/our-place-coffee) are both Main Street coffee stops, good for arrival or a mid-market break.

**Brunch** — [Rosie's Place](/businesses/rosies-place) is a long-standing brunch favorite on Main Street, well-suited for a Saturday morning meal after the market.

**Casual lunch** — [Greek's Pizzeria](/businesses/greeks-pizzeria) for pizza and Greek classics. [Zionsville Pizzeria](/businesses/zionsville-pizzeria) is another Main Street pizza option.

**Sweets** — [The Scoop](/businesses/the-scoop) is across from the market on South Main Street — a natural post-shopping stop on warm Saturdays.

**Take home** — [Angelo's Italian Market](/businesses/angelos-italian-market) carries housemade pasta, sauces, and prepared foods that pair well with whatever produce you found at the market.
