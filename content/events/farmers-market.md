The Zionsville Farmers Market is one of the most popular weekly gatherings in the Village, drawing locals and visitors to Main Street every Saturday morning from mid-May through late September. The market features a rotating mix of local vendors offering farm-fresh produce, artisanal breads, specialty meats, and fresh flowers, along with live music and community activities.

## What to expect

Vendors rotate throughout the season but typically include fresh produce from local farms, baked goods, prepared foods, honey, flowers, and handmade goods. Live music is common, making it as much a social event as a shopping trip.

## Plan your visit

**Hours:** 8:00 AM – 11:30 AM  
**Location:** 340 S Main St (along Main Street in the Village)  
**Parking:** Main Street is closed to vehicles during market hours. The closest parking lot is at the northwest corner of Main Street and Sycamore Rd., accessible via First Street.  

Parking is also available along Main Street north of the market area, on nearby side streets, and at Lions Park. Spaces close to the market fill quickly after 8:30, so if you prefer to park nearby, aim to arrive around 8:00 when it’s less crowded.

**Dogs:** Welcome on leash.

## Tips from regulars

Arrive before 9 AM for the best selection, especially for popular items like sweet corn and specialty baked goods. Many vendors sell out before closing. Bring a reusable bag and cash — most vendors accept cards, but cash is faster.

After the market, Main Street shops begin opening around 10 AM, making it easy to continue your morning downtown.
