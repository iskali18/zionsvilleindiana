Stories in the Park is a free family-friendly outdoor reading event hosted by the Zionsville Lions Club at Lions Park. Held annually on a Friday morning in early June, the event is geared toward children from preschool through fourth grade, though all children are welcome.

The Lions Club runs the morning as part of its broader literacy mission — the goal is to make reading fun and encourage a lifelong love of books in younger kids.

## What's planned for 2026

This year's morning includes a wide mix of literacy-focused activities:

- **Puppet show** featuring Clover the dog
- **Storytelling** and read-alouds
- **Brick Street Poetry Group** presentation
- **Craft projects**
- **Play acting**
- **Introduction to Braille**
- **Zionsville Parks exhibit**
- **Bookmobile** on-site
- **Free book** for each child from the Indy Book Project

## Plan your visit

**When:** Friday, June 5, 2026  
**Hours:** 10:00 AM – noon  
**Location:** Lions Park, 11053 Sycamore St, Zionsville  
**Admission:** Free  
**Parking:** Lions Park has its own parking lot off Sycamore Street.

Bring a blanket or lawn chairs — most of the morning happens on open grass at the park. Sunscreen and water are good ideas if the morning is sunny.

## After the event

Lions Park is adjacent to [Creekside Nature Park](/parks/creekside-nature-park), an easy short walk for families looking to extend the morning outdoors. Downtown Zionsville is also a short drive or walk away — [Rosie's Place](/businesses/rosies-place) is a popular family-friendly lunch spot, and [The Scoop](/businesses/the-scoop) on South Main Street is a natural treat stop for kids on warm days.
