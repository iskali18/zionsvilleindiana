Rosie's Place is a family-owned breakfast, brunch, and lunch café on South Main Street in the heart of the Village, open in Zionsville since 2014. The restaurant is named for the grandmother of owner Debbi Bourgerie, who runs it with her husband, Michael, and most items — baked goods, bread, dressings, and soups — are made from scratch. The menu covers all-day breakfast — including custom omelets, several eggs Benedict variations, pancakes, and breakfast burritos — alongside a lunch menu of sandwiches, salads, and house-made soups.

The bakery case is stocked daily with Gooey Butter Cookies in original, chocolate, and rotating seasonal flavors, the café's signature item and Rosie's own spin on the St. Louis Gooey Butter Cake. The cookies are sold under their own Gooey Butter Cookies label and ship nationwide. Weekend brunch includes cocktails such as mimosas, Bloody Marys, and a rotating seasonal mimosa.

Rosie's Place has locations in Zionsville, Noblesville, and Carmel. The Zionsville café is open Monday through Friday 7:00 AM to 3:00 PM and Saturday and Sunday 8:00 AM to 2:00 PM, so plan to visit earlier in the day.
