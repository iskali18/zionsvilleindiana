Lincoln Park occupies a prominent spot in the heart of Zionsville's Village district — both physically and historically. Located on Main Street, the park marks the site where Abraham Lincoln once delivered an address, and where the community gathered to honor his passing when the funeral train carrying his remains stopped in Zionsville.

## The 2026 Refresh

A renovation is underway with a spring 2026 start date and completion targeted for late 2026. Plans include drainage improvements, a new gazebo to replace the former structure (with electrical access), and accessibility upgrades. A commemorative brick program has reopened for community participation.

## What's here

Lincoln Park is a Village green in the traditional sense — a central gathering space rather than a recreation facility. It's best experienced as part of a broader downtown visit: a place to pause, sit, and take in the Village atmosphere.

## Historical significance

Zionsville was one of the stops on the route of Lincoln's funeral train following his assassination in April 1865. The community turned out to pay respects as the train passed through, and Lincoln Park preserves that connection to a significant moment in American history.
